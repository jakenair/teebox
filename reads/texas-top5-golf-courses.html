Where to Play

The 5 Best Golf Courses in Texas

TeeBox · July 9, 2026 · 5 min read

Texas golf runs deeper than most people think — piney-woods sanctuaries an hour outside Houston, a quarry-carved club in the middle of Dallas, and some of the most exclusive private retreats in the country. These are the five courses we'd build a trip around.

1. Whispering Pines
Trinity · Private

Consistently ranked the No. 1 course in Texas, Whispering Pines hides deep in the East Texas piney woods and plays like a private cathedral — towering trees, flawless conditioning, and not another soul in sight. Chet Williams routed it to reward precision over power, with water and sand framing greens that give up nothing cheaply. Getting on is famously hard; if the invite ever comes, clear your calendar.

2. Dallas National
Dallas · Private

Tom Fazio found dramatic elevation on a former quarry south of downtown and built the most exclusive club in the Metroplex on top of it. It’s long, muscular, and immaculately conditioned, with tumbling terrain you’d never expect to find inside Dallas city limits. The back nine plays through rock and water — big, bold, and unmistakably Fazio.

3. Childress Hall
Texas · Private

One of the state’s most closely held private retreats — the kind of place members guard and guests never forget. Wide, walkable, and built for pure golf, it rewards the ground game and asks you to leave the ego in the car. No tee sheet, no crowds — just golf the way it’s meant to feel.

4. Bluejack National
Montgomery · Private

Tiger Woods’ first design to open in the United States, an hour north of Houston. TGR Design cleared the corridors wide and firm so it plays fast and fun for members while still baring its teeth from the tips. Don’t miss The Playground — a ten-hole par-3 course with a walk-up snack shack that half the new clubs in America are now trying to copy.

5. Covey
Texas · Private

A newer name on the Texas scene, and proof the state’s private-club boom shows no signs of slowing. Understated and members-first, it’s built around wide fairways, bold greens, and the unhurried pace that makes a great club feel like a second home. The kind of place you join for thirty years, not thirty rounds.

From the piney woods to the big city, Texas hides some of the best golf in the country — you just have to know where to look.
