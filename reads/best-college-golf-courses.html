Where to Play

The 10 Best College Golf Courses

TeeBox · July 12, 2026 · 6 min read

Some of the best golf in the country sits right on a college campus — a C.B. Macdonald original at Yale, a pair of Alister MacKenzie designs at Michigan and Ohio State, a Tom Fazio championship track at Oklahoma State. These are the ten college courses we'd cross the country to play.

1. Yale
Yale Golf Course · New Haven, CT · C.B. Macdonald & Seth Raynor (1926)

The undisputed king of college golf. Macdonald and Raynor blasted this out of rugged Connecticut woodland and rock, and it plays like a museum of the great template holes — Redan, Biarritz, Cape — built at a scale nobody attempts anymore. The par-3 9th, a downhill Biarritz with a chasm of a swale running through a green the size of a tennis court, is one of the most photographed holes in America. If you only play one course on this list, play Yale.

2. Ohio State
Scarlet Course · Columbus, OH · Alister MacKenzie (1938)

Alister MacKenzie’s Midwest heavyweight, and the course where a young Jack Nicklaus learned to compete. A Nicklaus-led restoration in the 2000s brought back MacKenzie’s bold bunkering and tumbling green contours after decades of quiet softening. It’s long, muscular, and every bit a championship test — the closing stretch has settled plenty of Big Ten titles.

3. Stanford
Stanford Golf Course · Stanford, CA · George Thomas & Billy Bell (1930)

Tucked into the oak-studded foothills above campus, this is where Tiger Woods, Tom Watson, and Michelle Wie sharpened their games. George Thomas — of Riviera and L.A. North fame — routed it through dramatic elevation change, hiding a member’s course inside a course good enough to produce major champions. The back nine climbs into the hills and tumbles back down, with downhill par-4s that reward a well-judged, running tee ball.

4. Michigan
University of Michigan Golf Course · Ann Arbor, MI · Alister MacKenzie (1931)

The other MacKenzie on this list, and a short walk from the Big House. Built in the same window as Cypress Point and Augusta National, it packs the master’s genius — wild green complexes, cape-style bunkering, tempting angles — onto a compact, rolling site. Don’t let the modest yardage fool you: the greens punish a lazy approach every single time.

5. Oklahoma State
Karsten Creek · Stillwater, OK · Tom Fazio (1994)

The most feared course in college golf, full stop. Fazio threaded it through native blackjack oak and prairie, with water and forced carries lurking down the stretch — there’s a reason it keeps drawing the NCAA Championship. The closing holes play around a lake to greens tucked hard against the water, and they’ve wrecked more than a few national-title hopes.

6. Indiana
Pfau Course · Bloomington, IN · Steve Smyers redesign (2020)

The newcomer, and proof of how far a bold redesign can go. Smyers cleared out the old trees, widened the corridors, and reshaped the property into a firm, fast, links-leaning test that jumped straight onto the national rankings. Generous off the tee and brutal around the greens — exactly the modern, strategic direction the college game is heading.

7. Notre Dame
Warren Golf Course · Notre Dame, IN · Coore & Crenshaw (2000)

Bill Coore and Ben Crenshaw’s minimalist touch, laid out just off campus with the Golden Dome never far from view. Native fescue frames wide, ground-game-friendly fairways, and the routing rewards the player who thinks two shots ahead over the one who just bombs it. A quietly brilliant walk that gets better every time you play it.

8. Virginia Tech
Pete Dye River Course · Radford, VA · Pete Dye (2006)

Classic Pete Dye theater, strung along the banks of the New River. Railroad ties, deceptive angles, and water in play on a run of holes that demand nerve — this is a course that will happily hand you a big number the moment you flinch. The riverside stretch is as scenic as anything on the list, and twice as intimidating.

9. Wisconsin
University Ridge · Verona, WI · Robert Trent Jones Jr. (1991)

A course of two personalities: an open, prairie-style front nine that gives way to a tighter, wooded back through the Wisconsin hardwoods. RTJ Jr.’s design has enough teeth to host a PGA Tour Champions event alongside the Badgers. Firm, fair, and a genuinely fun daily-fee play for anyone passing through Madison.

10. Duke
Duke University Golf Club · Durham, NC · Robert Trent Jones / RTJ Jr. redesign (1994)

Robert Trent Jones Sr. laid it out in 1957; his son gave it a muscular redesign decades later. Set among the towering pines of the Carolina Piedmont, it’s a stout, traditional parkland test that also happens to be one of the best public-access university courses in the country. Book a tee time and the on-site Washington Duke Inn and make a weekend of it — a fitting anchor to a list this deep.

From Ivy League originals to modern championship tracks, the best college courses hold up against anything on the public or private lists — you just have to know where to look.
