Why Mizuno irons are a religion

If you ask a tour player or a teaching pro which irons feel best, the answer is almost always the same: Mizuno. The Hiroshima forge has been turning out Grain Flow Forged irons since the 1960s, and the carbon steel they use still produces a sound and feel on a flushed shot that nothing else really matches.

The current Pro 241, Pro 243, and Pro 245 split the player's iron market three ways — true muscle-back, compact cavity, and a slightly more forgiving shape. The JPX 925 line takes that forged feel and adds modern game-improvement help. The T24 wedges are some of the cleanest-shaped wedges in golf, and the ST drivers have quietly become very good.
