Why TaylorMade dominates the rack

TaylorMade has been pushing the driver category forward since they introduced the original metalwood in 1979 — and four decades later, they're still the brand most likely to put something genuinely new on the rack each season. The carbon-faced Stealth lineup rewrote what a driver looks like; the Qi10 family pushed MOI forward; and the P-Series irons quietly became the choice of more than half the tour.

But it's not just driver hype. TaylorMade's P790, P7MC, and P7CB irons cover everyone from low-handicappers wanting forged feel to mid-handicappers wanting forgiveness without playing a shovel. The MG (Milled Grind) wedges are some of the cleanest-shaped wedges on the market, and the Spider Tour line keeps showing up in the bags of major winners.
